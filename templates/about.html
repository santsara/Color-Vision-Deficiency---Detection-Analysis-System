About Color Vision Deficiency

What is CVD?

Color Vision Deficiency (CVD), commonly known as color blindness, is a condition in which individuals have difficulty distinguishing certain colors due to abnormalities in the cone cells of the retina. It most commonly affects red-green color perception.

Types of Color Vision Deficiency

Protanopia: Reduced sensitivity to red light, making red colors appear darker and harder to distinguish from green.

Deuteranopia: Reduced sensitivity to green light. This is the most common type of color vision deficiency.

Tritanopia: A rare condition affecting blue-yellow color discrimination.

How is it Detected?

Color Vision Deficiency is commonly screened using Ishihara Test Plates. These plates contain colored dot patterns forming numbers that are visible only to individuals with normal color vision. Difficulty identifying these numbers may indicate a specific type of color vision deficiency.

Important Note

This web application provides a digital screening tool for awareness and educational purposes only. It is not a substitute for professional medical diagnosis. For accurate assessment, consultation with a certified ophthalmologist is recommended.
